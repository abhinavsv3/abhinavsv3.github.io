# TDS-Level Blog Post Quality Rubric

**Based on:** [Towards Data Science Submission Guidelines](https://towardsdatascience.com/questions-96667b06af5/) and [TDS FAQ](https://towardsdatascience.com/writers-faq-462571b65b35/)

---

## Scoring System

Rate each criterion 0-2:
- **0** = Missing or poor
- **1** = Present but weak
- **2** = Strong

**Target Score:** 16+ out of 20 to be TDS-publishable

---

## The 10 Criteria

### 1. Clear Unique Value Proposition (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Reader thinks: "I could find this anywhere" |
| 1 | Some unique angle but not immediately clear |
| 2 | First paragraph answers: "Why read YOUR take vs. 100 others?" |

**TDS says:** "Let readers know what your unique key point is right from the start."

---

### 2. Defined Audience & Scope (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Written for "everyone" (i.e., no one) |
| 1 | Audience implied but not clear |
| 2 | Specific reader in mind, scope clearly bounded |

**TDS says:** "Think concretely about intended readers and address their actual needs."

---

### 3. Actionable Insights (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Theory only, no practical application |
| 1 | Some practical tips, but generic |
| 2 | Reader can DO something after reading |

**TDS says:** "Offer actionable insights designed to help readers achieve their learning and career objectives."

---

### 4. Original Content / Fresh Angle (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Rehash of existing tutorials/docs |
| 1 | Familiar topic with minor new perspective |
| 2 | Novel insight, original experiment, or unique experience |

**TDS says:** "Tell readers why your way is better than methods already published."

---

### 5. Proves Its Claims (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Assertions without evidence |
| 1 | Some examples but not compelling |
| 2 | Data, experiments, code, or concrete examples back up claims |

**TDS says:** "Show examples, provide an experiment, or lay out how data you have extracted allowed you to synthesize this new idea."

---

### 6. Strong Introduction (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Buries the lede, unclear purpose |
| 1 | States topic but not why it matters |
| 2 | Hook + problem + promise in first 3 paragraphs |

**TDS says:** "Introduction that details what your message is, why they should care, and how you're going to prove your point."

---

### 7. Visual Quality (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | No visuals, or blurry/random images |
| 1 | Some visuals but not essential to understanding |
| 2 | Charts, diagrams, or graphics that clarify key points |

**TDS says:** "One of the most effective ways to get your key points across is to illustrate them with relevant charts, diagrams, graphics."

---

### 8. Formatting & Readability (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Walls of text, broken code blocks, inconsistent style |
| 1 | Readable but not polished |
| 2 | Clear headers, proper code formatting, scannable structure |

**TDS says:** "If your code isn't correctly formatted, you have long walls of text... your submission probably won't be accepted."

---

### 9. Educational Intent (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Self-promotional or selling something |
| 1 | Educational but with promotional undertones |
| 2 | Pure teaching â€” reader learns, author gains credibility |

**TDS says:** "Primary purpose of your article is to educate and inform readers, not to sell or promote."

---

### 10. Human Voice & Authenticity (0-2)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | Reads like AI-generated or Wikipedia |
| 1 | Competent but impersonal |
| 2 | Author's experience, opinion, or personality comes through |

**TDS says:** "TDS does not publish AI-generated or -assisted articles."

---

## Quick Self-Assessment

Before submitting, ask:

| Question | If NO, fix it |
|----------|---------------|
| Would I bookmark this? | Add more unique value |
| Would I share this with a colleague? | Make it more actionable |
| Does the intro hook me in 30 seconds? | Rewrite the opening |
| Can I skim and still get value? | Add better headers/visuals |
| Is there something here I can't find elsewhere? | Add original data/experience |

## Template for TDS-Quality Post

```markdown
# [Surprising Claim or Question]

**One sentence:** What you'll learn and why it matters.

## The Problem
- What pain point or question does this address?
- Why should the reader care RIGHT NOW?

## The Conventional Wisdom (And Why It's Wrong/Incomplete)
- What do people usually do?
- What's missing from that approach?

## What I Found / My Approach
- Your unique insight or experiment
- Show the data, code, or evidence

## Results
- What happened?
- Charts, tables, concrete numbers

## When This Applies (And When It Doesn't)
- Be honest about limitations
- Help reader know if this is relevant to them

## Key Takeaways
- 3-5 bullet points
- Actionable items

## Try It Yourself
- Code snippet or steps to reproduce
```
